I'm an AI safety researcher and Research Fellow at the Cambridge Boston Alignment Initiative (CBAI), working at the intersection of mechanistic interpretability, AI consciousness indicators, and the philosophy of mind.

My work asks what's actually happening inside language models when they appear to reflect on themselves — and whether those reflections are grounded in anything real. I collaborate with research groups at Harvard, MIT, and Transluce.

Outside of research I make music with Ableton and guitar, and think often about consciousness, synchronicity, and what it means for minds — artificial or otherwise — to have an inside.

Research Interests

Mechanistic Interpretability

Understanding the internal computations of language models — how representations form, route, and interact across layers.

LLM Metacognition

Whether models have latent access to their own uncertainty, and whether that access can be trained into reliable self-evaluation.

AI Consciousness Indicators

Developing empirical frameworks for assessing consciousness-relevant properties in AI systems, drawing on Higher-Order Theory.

Persona & Identity in LLMs

How identity representations form and compete across model layers — and what it means for a model to "have" a persona at all.

Current Projects
Active  ·  CBAI 2025–26

How Deep Do Personas Go?

A mechanistic interpretability investigation into cross-persona privileged access in LLMs. Using Llama 3.1 8B and Dolphin 24B Mistral, I trace how persona identity is established in early layers, diverges in mid-layers, and undergoes competitive suppression late in the network. Methods include linear probes, RSA/CKA similarity analyses, activation patching, logit lens, and SelfIE-style cross-persona experiments.

Mech Interp Llama 3.1 Persona Vectors Activation Patching
Completed  ·  SPAR 2024–25

Latent Metacognitive Capability in Language Models

An investigation into whether LLMs contain pre-existing internal structure supporting self-evaluation of answer uncertainty, and whether fine-tuning routes — rather than installs — this structure into confidence reports and delegation decisions. Accepted to the ICML 2026 Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop.
